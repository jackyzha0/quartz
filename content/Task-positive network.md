## Task-positive network # 

The task-positive network is a [[brain]] network primarily composed of the [[IPS]] and [[frontal eye fields]]. It is named and most known for its role in voluntary orienting of visuospatial attention. As the [[IPS]] and [[frontal eye fields]] were noticed to be activated during many attention-demanding tasks. They sit in a kind of see-saw relationship with each other; when one is active, the other goes quiet, and vice versa.
